PIPA Program

Partners in Policymaking Alabama — Training for Disability Policy Advocates

What is PIPA?

PIPA is a comprehensive 10-week training program delivered by UAB's Center for Excellence in Developmental Disabilities. It's designed for people with disabilities, family members, and professionals who want to develop advocacy and policy leadership skills.

📖

Core Curriculum

Disability history, federal and state policy frameworks, how bills become law, and advocacy strategy.

🎤

Communication Skills

Testimony, elevator pitches, negotiation, media engagement, and working with elected officials.

🤝

Networking

Connect with advocates, policymakers, and disability leaders across Alabama and nationally.

✅

Cohort-Based

Learn with peers who share your passion for change. Build lasting professional relationships.

UCEED Resources

University Centers on Educating All Learners with Disabilities — National Network & Tools

What is UCEED?

The Association of University Centers on Disability (AUCD) network includes University Centers across the US dedicated to improving the quality of life for individuals with disabilities through research, training, and service delivery. UAB is home to one of these centers.

🔬

Research & Innovation

University-led research on disability inclusion, education, employment, and policy.

👨‍🎓

Training & Education

Professional development, leadership programs, and community education initiatives.

💼

Employment & Transition

Career development, internship programs, and transition planning support.

🌐

National Network

Connect with university disability centers, researchers, and practitioners nationwide.
